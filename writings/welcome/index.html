Welcome to the ZFordDev Knowledge Library

Sun Jan 12 2025 10:00:00 GMT+1000 (Australian Eastern Standard Time)

SnapDock Engine v

Every project I build starts the same way: with notes.
Not polished documentation, not a grand plan — just raw thoughts, sketches, fragments, and the occasional “future me will understand this” lie.

Over time, I’ve realised those notes aren’t just a by‑product of the work.
They are the work.

They’re where ideas form, where problems get untangled, and where the real engineering happens long before a line of code exists. My process has always been built on planning, reflection, and writing things down so I can understand them better — and so I can return later with clearer eyes.

This section of the site exists to give those notes a home.

Right now, it’s empty. That’s intentional.
It’s a blank space waiting to be filled with the things that matter:

  • the lessons learned while building tools
  • the patterns that keep showing up
  • the small discoveries that deserve to be written down
  • the decisions that shape how my projects grow
  • the ideas that don’t fit anywhere else yet

As new projects evolve, as problems get solved, and as I refine the way I build, this library will grow alongside everything else. Some entries will be short. Some will be deep dives. Some will be reminders to myself that might help someone else one day.

This isn’t the blog.
That’s coming later, with its own tone, purpose, and direction.

The Knowledge Library is different.
It’s the backbone — a place for reference material, internal logic, engineering notes, and the thinking that supports everything I build under the ZFordDev name.
