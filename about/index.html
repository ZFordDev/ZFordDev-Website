About Me

I'm a builder obsessed with creating elegant solutions to complex problems. Every artifact you see here represents a journey through design, engineering, and the relentless pursuit of better systems.

Who I Am

I'm a full-stack engineer and product builder based at ZetoLabs. My work spans from low-level systems programming to high-level product design, always with an emphasis on local-first architectures and user agency.

I believe in building tools that respect user privacy, operate without cloud dependencies, and empower creators to extend and customize their workflows.

My Philosophy

Artifact-Driven Development

Each project is a tangible artifact—not just code, but a complete system designed to solve real problems for real users.

Local-First Architecture

Privacy by design. Data lives on your machine. Your tools should work offline and sync when you choose.

Modular Systems

Software should be composable. Users and developers should be able to extend, modify, and reimagine systems without limitations.

What I Build

I work across the full spectrum of software development:

  • Desktop Applications — Electron and Tauri for cross-platform desktop tools
  • Systems Programming — Rust for performance-critical, secure systems
  • AI & Automation — Modular agents and workflow orchestration
  • Infrastructure — Kubernetes, networking, and homelab systems
  • Game Development — Unreal Engine and interactive experiences

Why I Build

The world is full of tools that collect your data, require constant internet, and restrict what you can do. I build to change that.

Every artifact here is a statement: "This is how software should be built." Fast. Modular. Respectful. Extensible.

Whether you're using SnapDock to take notes, ZIA to augment your workflow with AI, NestKey to keep your passwords safe, or exploring Thronebound's world, you're using systems designed with you—not against you.
